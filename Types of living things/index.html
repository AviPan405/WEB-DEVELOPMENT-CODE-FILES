The 5 classifications of Vertabrates

The five main classifications of vertebrates (animals with backbones) are mammals, birds, fish, reptiles, and amphibians. These groups are distinguished by characteristics such as skin covering, reproduction methods, breathing mechanisms, and body temperature regulation (warm-blooded vs. cold-blooded).


Vertebrates are animals that possess a backbone or spinal column, including mammals, birds, reptiles, amphibians, and fish. They are characterized by having an internal skeleton (endoskeleton), a nervous system, and a distinct head with a brain, making up less than 5% of all known animal species

Warm-blooded, egg-laying vertebrates with feathers, wings, and lightweight bones (e.g., eagles, penguins, parrots).


Cold-blooded aquatic vertebrates with scales, fins, and gills for breathing underwater (e.g., sharks, salmon, tuna).


Cold-blooded, air-breathing vertebrates covered in scales or scutes, typically laying leathery eggs on land (e.g., snakes, turtles, lizards).


Cold-blooded vertebrates that live part of their life in water (breathing with gills) and part on land (breathing with lungs), with smooth, moist skin (e.g., frogs, salamanders).
