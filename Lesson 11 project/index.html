The 5 Major Forms of Literature

Literature is a form of art that uses language to express ideas, emotions, and stories. It encompasses a wide range of genres and styles, each with its own unique characteristics and purposes. Here are the five major forms of literature:


  • Fiction Prose: Imaginary stories written in ordinary language (e.g., novels, short stories).
  • Non-Fiction Prose: Based on real events and facts (e.g., biographies, history books).
  • Poetry: A form of literature that uses rhythmic and aesthetic qualities of language to evoke emotions and ideas.
  • Dramatic Literature: Written for performance on stage (e.g., plays).
  • Folktale: Traditional stories passed down through generations (e.g., fairy tales, myths).
